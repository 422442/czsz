Important Concepts

1. Slope of Isothermal and Adiabatic Curve

Isothermal Process

For an isothermal process, temperature is constant.

From the ideal gas equation:

\[PV = nRT\]

If \(T\) is constant, then:

\[PV = \text{Constant}\]

Differentiating with respect to \(V\):

\[P\,dV + V\,dP = 0\] \[V\,dP = -P\,dV\]

The slope of the P-V curve is \(\frac{dP}{dV}\):

\[\text{Slope}_{\text{isothermal}} = \frac{dP}{dV} = -\frac{P}{V}\]
Adiabatic Process

For an adiabatic process:

\[PV^\gamma = \text{Constant}\]

Differentiating with respect to \(V\):

\[d(PV^\gamma) = dP(V^\gamma) + P(\gamma V^{\gamma-1}\,dV) = 0\] \[V^\gamma\,dP = -\gamma P V^{\gamma-1}\,dV\]

The slope of the P-V curve is:

\[\text{Slope}_{\text{adiabatic}} = \frac{dP}{dV} = -\gamma\frac{P V^{\gamma-1}}{V^\gamma} = -\gamma\frac{P}{V}\]
Conclusion
\[\text{Slope}_{\text{adiabatic}} = \gamma \times \left(-\frac{P}{V}\right) = \gamma \times \text{Slope}_{\text{isothermal}}\]

Since \(\gamma = 1 + \frac{2}{f}\) (or \(\frac{C_p}{C_v}\)) and is always greater than 1 (\(\gamma > 1\)), the adiabatic curve is always steeper than the isothermal curve at any given point.

Important Note: Adiabatic curve ka slope hamesha jada hota hai as compared to Isothermal.

Gamma values:

  • Monoatomic: \(\gamma = 1. 6\)
  • Diatomic: \(\gamma = 1.4\)
  • Triatomic: \(\gamma = 1.3\)

Steepness: Mono > Dia > Tria

2. Bulk Modulus of Gas

Bulk Modulus (B) is defined as:

\[B = -\frac{dP}{(dV/V)} = -V\frac{dP}{dV}\]
Isothermal Bulk Modulus (\(B_{\text{iso}}\))

We know for an isothermal process, \(\frac{dP}{dV} = -\frac{P}{V}\).

\[B_{\text{iso}} = -V\left(-\frac{P}{V}\right) = P\]
Adiabatic Bulk Modulus (\(B_{\text{adia}}\))

We know for an adiabatic process, \(\frac{dP}{dV} = -\gamma\frac{P}{V}\).

\[B_{\text{adia}} = -V\left(-\gamma\frac{P}{V}\right) = \gamma P\]
Conclusion
\[B_{\text{adiabatic}} = \gamma \cdot B_{\text{isothermal}}\]

Also, Bulk Modulus is the reciprocal of compressibility:

\[B = \frac{1}{\text{Compressibility}}\]

Note: Adiabatic processes are often "sudden" processes.

3. Concept of Mixing of Gases (Non-Reacting)

Let two gases with moles \(n_1, n_2\), specific heats \(C_{V1}, C_{P1}, C_{V2}, C_{P2}\) and adiabatic exponents \(\gamma_1, \gamma_2\) be mixed.

a) Total Internal Energy of Mixture:
\[U_{\text{Total mix}} = U_1 + U_2\]
b) Molar Specific Heat of Mixture:

The total internal energy change is the sum of individual changes:

\[(n_1 + n_2)C_{V_{\text{mix}}}\,dT = n_1 C_{V1}\,dT + n_2 C_{V2}\,dT\]
\[C_{V_{\text{mix}}} = \frac{n_1 C_{V1} + n_2 C_{V2}}{n_1 + n_2}\]

Similarly, for constant pressure:

\[C_{P_{\text{mix}}} = \frac{n_1 C_{P1} + n_2 C_{P2}}{n_1 + n_2}\]

The adiabatic exponent for the mixture is:

\[\gamma_{\text{mix}} = \frac{C_{P_{\text{mix}}}}{C_{V_{\text{mix}}}}\]

Practice Questions

QUESTION 01: Conversion of Graphs

A cyclic process ABCD is shown in the P-V diagram. Which of the following curves represent the same process?

Analysis of P-V diagram:
  • A → B: Isobaric process (P = constant), Volume increases. Since \(V \propto T\), Temperature increases.
  • B → C: Not a standard process. Pressure decreases, Volume increases.
  • C → D: Isochoric process (V = constant), Pressure decreases. Since \(P \propto T\), Temperature decreases.
  • D → A: Not a standard process.
Analysis of P-T options:
  • A → B: P is constant, T increases. This is a horizontal line moving to the right.
  • C → D: V is constant. Since \(P \propto T\), this is a straight line passing through the origin. Pressure decreases, so temperature must also decrease.
Correct Answer: Option 1
QUESTION 02

A cyclic process ABCA is shown in P-T diagram. When presented on P-V, it would?

Analysis of P-T diagram:
  • A → B: Isobaric process (P = constant), Temperature increases. From \(V \propto T\), Volume increases. This is a horizontal line on a P-V diagram.
  • B → C: Isothermal process (T = constant). From \(PV = \text{constant}\), \(P \propto \frac{1}{V}\). This is a rectangular hyperbola. Pressure decreases, so Volume increases.
  • C → A: V = constant process. Since \(P \propto T\), pressure decreases as temperature decreases. This is a vertical line on a P-V diagram.
Correct Answer: Option 3 (Isobaric expansion → Isothermal expansion → Isochoric pressure drop)
QUESTION 03

A cyclic process ABCA is shown in the V-T diagram. Process on the P-V diagram is?

Analysis of V-T diagram:
  • A → B: \(V \propto T\). This is a line through the origin, representing an Isobaric process (P = constant). Volume increases.
  • B → C: Isochoric process (V = constant). Temperature decreases. From \(P \propto T\), pressure decreases.
  • C → A: Isothermal process (T = constant). Volume decreases.
Correct Answer: Option 3 (Isobaric expansion → Isochoric pressure drop → Isothermal compression)
QUESTION 04 (JEE Main-2017)

\(C_p\) and \(C_v\) are specific heats at constant pressure and constant volume respectively. It is observed that \(C_p - C_v = a\) for hydrogen gas and \(C_p - C_v = b\) for nitrogen gas. The correct relation between a and b is:

  1. a = 14b
  2. a = 28b
  3. a = 1/14b
  4. a = b
Solution:

The relation for molar specific heats is \(C_P - C_V = R\), which is independent of the gas.

The question gives specific heats (per unit mass), denoted by lowercase \(c_p, c_v\). The relation is:

\[c_p - c_v = \frac{R}{M}\]

where M is the molar mass.

For Hydrogen (\(H_2\)): M = 2 g/mol

\[a = c_p - c_v = \frac{R}{2}\]

For Nitrogen (\(N_2\)): M = 28 g/mol

\[b = c_p - c_v = \frac{R}{28}\]

To find the relation between a and b:

\[\frac{a}{b} = \frac{R/2}{R/28} = \frac{28}{2} = 14\] \[a = 14b\]
Correct Answer: 1 (a = 14b)
QUESTION 05

The volume of a diatomic gas with \(\gamma = 1.5\) is changed adiabatically from 16 ltrs to 12 ltrs. Find the ratio of final and initial pressure and temperature.

Solution:

Given: \(\gamma = 1.5 = 3/2\), \(V_i = 16\) L, \(V_f = 12\) L.

Pressure Ratio:

For an adiabatic process, \(P_i V_i^\gamma = P_f V_f^\gamma\).

\[\frac{P_f}{P_i} = \left(\frac{V_i}{V_f}\right)^\gamma = \left(\frac{16}{12}\right)^{3/2} = \left(\frac{4}{3}\right)^{3/2} = \frac{\sqrt{4^3}}{\sqrt{3^3}} = \frac{8}{3\sqrt{3}}\]
Temperature Ratio:

For an adiabatic process, \(T_i V_i^{\gamma-1} = T_f V_f^{\gamma-1}\).

\[\frac{T_f}{T_i} = \left(\frac{V_i}{V_f}\right)^{\gamma-1} = \left(\frac{16}{12}\right)^{0.5} = \left(\frac{4}{3}\right)^{0.5} = \sqrt{\frac{4}{3}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\]
QUESTION 06

A sample of 2kg of He is taken through the process ABC and another sample of 2Kg of same gas is taken through the process ADC as shown. How much is heat absorbed in process ABC and ADC.

Given Data:
  • Gas: He (Monoatomic), M = 4 g/mol, f = 3
  • \(C_V = \frac{3}{2}R\), \(C_P = \frac{5}{2}R\)
  • Mass = 2 kg = 2000 g
  • Number of moles, \(n = \frac{2000}{4} = 500\) mol
From P-V diagram:
  • A: \((10 \text{ m}^3, 5 \times 10^4 \text{ N/m}^2)\)
  • B: \((10 \text{ m}^3, 10 \times 10^4 \text{ N/m}^2)\)
  • C: \((20 \text{ m}^3, 10 \times 10^4 \text{ N/m}^2)\)
  • D: \((20 \text{ m}^3, 5 \times 10^4 \text{ N/m}^2)\)
Process ABC:

A → B (Isochoric heating):

\[\Delta Q_{AB} = \frac{C_V}{R}(P_B - P_A)V_A = 1.5 \times 5 \times 10^5 = 7.5 \times 10^5 \text{ J}\]

B → C (Isobaric expansion):

\[\Delta Q_{BC} = \frac{C_P}{R}P_B(V_C - V_B) = 2. 5 \times 10 \times 10^5 = 25 \times 10^5 \text{ J}\]
Total Heat for ABC: \(\Delta Q_{ABC} = 32.5 \times 10^5\) J
Process ADC:

Using First Law: \(\Delta Q_{ADC} = \Delta U_{ADC} + W_{ADC}\)

Internal Energy Change:

\[\Delta U_{ADC} = 1.5 \times 15 \times 10^5 = 22.5 \times 10^5 \text{ J}\]

Work Done:

\[W_{ADC} = 5 \times 10^5 \text{ J}\]
Total Heat for ADC: \(\Delta Q_{ADC} = 27. 5 \times 10^5\) J
QUESTION 08

Two moles of an ideal gas at temperature \(T_0 = 300\) K were cooled isochorically so that gas pressure reduced by n = 2 times. Then by isobaric process the gas expands till its temperature gets back to its initial value. Find the total heat absorbed in this process.

Process Breakdown:

Initial State (1): \(P_0, V_0, T_0 = 300\) K, \(n = 2\) mol

Isochoric Cooling (1 → 2):

  • \(V_2 = V_0\), \(P_2 = P_0/2\)
  • Since \(P \propto T\) for isochoric, \(T_2 = T_0/2 = 150\) K
  • Heat exchanged: \(\Delta Q_1 = nC_V\Delta T = 2C_V(150 - 300) = -300C_V\) (Heat rejected)

Isobaric Heating (2 → 3):

  • \(P_3 = P_2 = P_0/2\), \(T_3 = T_0 = 300\) K
  • Heat exchanged: \(\Delta Q_2 = nC_P\Delta T = 2C_P(300 - 150) = 300C_P\) (Heat absorbed)
Total Heat Absorbed:
\[\Delta Q_{\text{Total}} = -300C_V + 300C_P = 300(C_P - C_V) = 300R\]
Total Heat Absorbed: 300R
QUESTION 10 (HC Verma)

Consider the cyclic process ABCA as shown. An ideal gas 2 moles undergoes this. A total of 1200 J heat is rejected by the gas in the complete cycle. Find the work done by the gas during process BC.

Given Data:
  • \(n = 2\) mol
  • \(\Delta Q_{\text{cycle}} = -1200\) J (heat is rejected)
  • For a cyclic process, \(\Delta U_{\text{cycle}} = 0\)

From First Law: \(\Delta Q_{\text{cycle}} = W_{\text{cycle}} \Rightarrow W_{\text{cycle}} = -1200\) J

Analysis of Processes:
  • A → B (Isobaric): \(W_{AB} = nR(T_B - T_A) = 2 \times 8.314 \times (500 - 300) = 3325.6\) J
  • C → A (Isochoric): \(W_{CA} = 0\)

Total Work: \(W_{\text{cycle}} = W_{AB} + W_{BC} + W_{CA}\)

\[-1200 = 3325.6 + W_{BC}\] \[W_{BC} = -1200 - 3325.6 = -4525.6 \text{ J}\]
Work Done BC: -4525.6 J
QUESTION 13 (JEE Main-2020)

An engine operates by taking a monatomic ideal gas through the cycle shown in the figure. The percentage efficiency of the engine is close to...

Cycle Analysis:

A → B (Isochoric Heating):

\[\Delta Q_{AB} = 3P_0V_0\]

B → C (Isobaric Expansion):

\[\Delta Q_{BC} = 7. 5P_0V_0\]

Total Heat Absorbed:

\[Q_{\text{in}} = 3P_0V_0 + 7.5P_0V_0 = 10.5P_0V_0\]

Work Done:

\[W = \text{Area of rectangle} = (3P_0 - P_0)(2V_0 - V_0) = 2P_0V_0\]
Efficiency:
\[\eta = \frac{W}{Q_{\text{in}}} \times 100 = \frac{2P_0V_0}{10.5P_0V_0} \times 100 = \frac{2}{10.5} \times 100 \approx 19\%\]
Efficiency: ≈ 19%
