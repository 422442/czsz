  • Logical: Software & Protocols
  • Information: Data & Content
  • People: Users & Admins

Unit B

Network Defence Tools

Tools and techniques used to protect network infrastructure from unauthorized access.

Firewalls

A security device that monitors traffic and decides whether to allow or block it based on rules.

Stateless: Inspects packets in isolation. Fast but less secure.
Stateful: Remembers the context of connections. Smarter and more secure.

VPN

Virtual Private Networks create an encrypted "tunnel" over the public internet, masking your IP and protecting data.

> Establishing secure tunnel...
> Encrypting data packets...
> Connection Secure.

Snort (IDS)

An open-source Network Intrusion Detection System. It acts like a digital burglar alarm, matching traffic against known attack signatures.

  • Packet Sniffing
  • Traffic Logging
  • Real-time Alerts

Investigating the dark side of the web: crimes, attackers, and how we catch them.

Phishing

Sending fraudulent emails pretending to be reputable companies to induce individuals to reveal personal information.

Malware

"Malicious Software" designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system.

Hacking

Identifying weakness in computer systems or networks to exploit its weaknesses to gain access.

Unit D

Security Landscape

Mapping the terminology and advanced threats facing nations and organizations.

Key Terminology

Threat A potential danger (e.g., hacker, virus).
Vulnerability A weakness or gap in security (e.g., weak password).
Risk The potential for loss when a threat exploits a vulnerability.

Cyber Warfare

Internet-based conflict involving politically motivated attacks by a nation-state on another nation. The goal is often sabotage or espionage.

Critical Infrastructure

Securing assets essential for society, such as power grids, water supplies, and hospitals. Attacks here can cause physical harm.

Unit E

Device Security & Best Practices

Practical steps to secure endpoints, mobiles, and personal data.

Password Policy

Complex passwords, no reuse, and enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

Patch Management

Regularly update OS and software to fix security holes.

Data Backup

The 3-2-1 Rule: 3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite.
